2-Day Lake Naivasha Escape

📅 Aug 22 – 23, 2026 ⏱ 2 Days / 1 Night 👥 Group Tour 🇰🇪 Kenya

Tour Overview

Lake Naivasha is one of Kenya's most beautiful freshwater lakes — a sparkling blue expanse set in the floor of the Great Rift Valley, fringed by yellow fever trees and papyrus beds, and home to an extraordinary variety of birdlife and wildlife. Just 90 minutes from Nairobi, it's the perfect quick escape from the city — close enough for a weekend trip but far enough to feel completely removed from urban life.

The lake's most famous feature is Crescent Island — a private wildlife sanctuary set on a peninsula jutting into the lake, where you can walk freely among zebra, wildebeest, giraffe, and impala with no fences and no vehicles. It's one of the only places in Kenya where you can walk alongside wildlife on foot in the open — a genuinely unique experience. Add a boat ride among hippo pods and hundreds of flamingos, and you have a packed two-day adventure at one of Kenya's most underrated destinations.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1
Nairobi → Lake Naivasha — Boat Ride & Crescent Island Walk

Depart Nairobi at 8:00am by road (approx. 90 minutes via the scenic Mai Mahiu escarpment road with stunning Rift Valley views). Arrive at the lake and board a traditional wooden boat for a guided hippo-watching cruise — the lake is home to one of Kenya's largest hippo populations, and they're frequently seen wallowing in the shallows and grazing on the lake's water hyacinth. Cross to Crescent Island for a guided walking safari — stroll among zebra, wildebeest, Thomson's gazelle, giraffe, and impala in complete freedom. Return to your lakeside lodge for lunch and a relaxed afternoon on the lake shore. Sundowner boat ride at dusk — flamingos gather in their thousands as the Rift Valley escarpment turns golden.

Day 2
Hell's Gate Morning Visit → Return to Nairobi

After an early breakfast, drive to the neighbouring Hell's Gate National Park for a morning cycling safari through the park's dramatic gorge (bikes included). Hell's Gate is one of Kenya's most unique parks — you cycle and walk freely among zebra, buffalo, and giraffe on open paths through volcanic rock formations and geothermal steam vents. After the cycling safari, return to Naivasha for lunch before departing for Nairobi. Arrive Nairobi by mid-afternoon.
