7-Day Namibia Desert Expedition

📅 Sep 14 – 20, 2026 ⏱ 7 Days / 6 Nights 👥 Small Group Tour 🇳🇦 Namibia

Tour Overview

Namibia is Africa's most otherworldly destination — a vast, ancient land of towering red sand dunes, bleached desert plains, wild Atlantic coastlines, and some of the darkest, starriest skies on earth. The Namib Desert is the world's oldest desert, and its iconic dunes at Sossusvlei — some reaching over 300 metres — are among the most photographed landscapes on the planet. This 7-day expedition takes you through the highlights of this extraordinary country, from the surreal landscapes of Sossusvlei and the haunting Deadvlei salt pan to the wildlife-rich Etosha National Park and the eerie shipwrecks of the Skeleton Coast.

Namibia is a destination for travellers who want something genuinely different — vast open spaces, extraordinary geology, unique desert-adapted wildlife, and a profound sense of solitude that is increasingly rare in the modern world. September is an ideal time to visit — dry season means excellent wildlife viewing at Etosha's waterholes, and the dunes glow their most vivid red in the morning and evening light.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1
Nairobi → Windhoek — Arrival & City Tour

Fly from Nairobi to Windhoek Hosea Kutako International Airport (approx. 4 hours, usually via Johannesburg). Transfer to your Windhoek hotel. Afternoon orientation tour of Namibia's compact and charming capital — the Christuskirche Lutheran church, the Alte Feste colonial fort, and the lively Independence Avenue. Welcome dinner at a Namibian restaurant featuring local specialties including oryx steak and kapana street meat.

Day 2
Windhoek → Sossusvlei — Drive Through the Desert

Early morning departure south towards the Namib Desert (approx. 5 hours drive through dramatic open landscapes). Stop at the Tropic of Capricorn marker en route. Check in to your desert lodge near Sesriem. Afternoon visit to Sesriem Canyon — a narrow gorge carved by the Tsauchab River over millions of years. Sundowner drinks watching the dunes change colour as the sun sets. Dinner under a sky blazing with stars.

Day 3
Sossusvlei — Dune 45, Big Daddy & Deadvlei

Pre-dawn departure to enter the park at sunrise — the best light for photography and the coolest temperatures for climbing. Climb Dune 45 — one of the most accessible of Sossusvlei's iconic red dunes — for panoramic views across the desert. Continue to Big Daddy, the tallest dune in the area, and descend into the surreal Deadvlei clay pan below — a blindingly white salt flat dotted with ancient, blackened camel thorn trees that died over 900 years ago but never decomposed in the arid air. One of Africa's most extraordinary landscapes. Return to lodge for breakfast and rest during the midday heat.

Day 4
Skeleton Coast Drive & Swakopmund

Drive northwest towards the Atlantic coast through the vast gravel plains of the Namib. Stop at Welwitschia Drive to see the ancient welwitschia plants — some over 1,500 years old — found nowhere else on earth. Arrive in Swakopmund — Namibia's charming coastal town with a distinctive German colonial architecture. Afternoon at leisure on the Atlantic Ocean beach. Optional quad biking or sandboarding on the coastal dunes (additional cost). Dinner at a Swakopmund seafood restaurant.

Day 5
Skeleton Coast → Etosha National Park

Drive north along the Skeleton Coast — Namibia's wild, fog-shrouded Atlantic shoreline strewn with the rusted wrecks of ships that ran aground in the treacherous surf, plus colonies of Cape fur seals at Cape Cross. Continue inland to Etosha National Park — one of Africa's great wildlife sanctuaries. Afternoon game drive around Etosha's famous waterholes where elephant, lion, cheetah, black rhino, giraffe, and oryx gather to drink in the dry season. Overnight at a lodge inside the park.

Day 6
Etosha National Park — Full Day Game Drives

A full day exploring Etosha's network of game drive routes and waterholes. Dawn drive when predators are active — lion, cheetah, and leopard are regularly sighted around the park's central waterholes. Etosha is particularly famous for its large herds of elephant and its black rhino population, which come to drink at floodlit waterholes after dark. Evening game drive with spotlight (available from the lodge). Dinner under the African sky.

Day 7
Etosha → Windhoek → Return to Nairobi

Final morning game drive before breakfast. Drive back to Windhoek (approx. 4 hours). Brief stop at a Namibian craft market for souvenirs. Transfer to Hosea Kutako International Airport for return flight to Nairobi via Johannesburg. Arrive Nairobi late evening.
