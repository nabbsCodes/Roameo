7-Day Cairo & Luxor Discovery

📅 Nov 5 – 11, 2026 ⏱ 7 Days / 6 Nights 👥 Group Tour 🇪🇬 Egypt

Tour Overview

Egypt is the cradle of one of the world's greatest civilisations — and nowhere is this more viscerally felt than standing before the Great Pyramid of Giza, the only surviving wonder of the ancient world. This 7-day discovery takes you through the two great centres of ancient Egyptian civilisation: Cairo in the north, where the pharaohs built their eternal monuments on the edge of the desert, and Luxor in the south — once the ancient city of Thebes and the world's greatest open-air museum, home to the Valley of the Kings, Karnak Temple, and the Colossi of Memnon.

November is one of the best months to visit Egypt — temperatures have dropped from the searing summer heat to a comfortable 25–28°C, making it ideal for exploring outdoor archaeological sites. Add a felucca sail on the Nile at sunset and a visit to the Egyptian Museum's extraordinary collection of ancient artefacts, and you have one of the most rewarding cultural journeys in the world.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1
Nairobi → Cairo — Arrival & Khan el-Khalili Bazaar

Fly from Nairobi to Cairo International Airport (approx. 5 hours). Transfer to your Cairo hotel near the city centre. Afternoon visit to the Khan el-Khalili bazaar — Cairo's ancient market district, a labyrinth of narrow alleys filled with spice vendors, gold jewellers, papyrus sellers, and traditional coffee houses that have been trading here since the 14th century. Welcome dinner at a Nile-view restaurant.

Day 2
Giza Pyramids, Sphinx & Egyptian Museum

The centrepiece of the entire trip. Morning visit to the Giza Plateau — stand before the Great Pyramid of Khufu, the Pyramid of Khafre, and the Pyramid of Menkaure, built over 4,500 years ago and still the most monumental structures ever created by human hands. Visit the enigmatic Great Sphinx. Afternoon at the Egyptian Museum in Tahrir Square — home to the world's largest collection of ancient Egyptian artefacts, including Tutankhamun's golden death mask and the Royal Mummy Room. Dinner in Cairo.

Day 3
Cairo → Luxor — Flight & Karnak Temple

Morning domestic flight from Cairo to Luxor (1 hour). Check in to your Luxor hotel on the East Bank of the Nile. Afternoon visit to the Karnak Temple Complex — the largest religious building ever constructed, a vast ancient city of temples, sphinxes, obelisks, and hypostyle halls built over 2,000 years by successive pharaohs. Evening stroll along Luxor's elegant Corniche beside the Nile.

Day 4
Valley of the Kings & West Bank

Cross to the West Bank of the Nile — the land of the dead in ancient Egyptian cosmology — for a full day in one of the world's most extraordinary archaeological zones. Visit the Valley of the Kings where 63 royal tombs are carved into the desert cliffs, including the tomb of Tutankhamun. Visit the Temple of Hatshepsut — the magnificent mortuary temple of ancient Egypt's most famous female pharaoh. See the Colossi of Memnon — two enormous stone statues that have stood sentinel on the Luxor plain for 3,400 years.

Day 5
Luxor Temple, Felucca Nile Cruise & Aswan Day Trip

Morning visit to Luxor Temple — a stunning complex right in the heart of modern Luxor, illuminated at night and connected to Karnak by the 3km Avenue of Sphinxes. Afternoon felucca sailing on the Nile — a traditional Egyptian wooden sailboat drifting past sugar cane fields and ancient temples in the golden afternoon light. Optional day trip to Aswan to see the Philae Temple and the High Dam (additional cost).

Day 6
Luxor → Cairo — Return & Islamic Cairo

Morning flight back to Cairo. Afternoon exploration of Islamic Cairo — visit the Citadel of Saladin and the stunning Mohammed Ali Mosque with its sweeping views over the city. Walk through the historic Al-Muizz Street, one of the oldest streets in Cairo, lined with medieval Islamic architecture. Farewell dinner at a traditional Egyptian restaurant.

Day 7
Cairo — Leisure Morning → Return to Nairobi

Final morning at leisure — visit the Grand Egyptian Museum near Giza (the world's largest archaeological museum, recently opened) or explore Cairo's modern neighbourhoods. Transfer to Cairo International Airport for return flight to Nairobi.
