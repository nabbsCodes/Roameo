4-Day Laikipia Conservancy Safari

📅 Sep 19 – 22, 2026 ⏱ 4 Days / 3 Nights 👥 Small Group Tour 🇰🇪 Kenya

Tour Overview

The Laikipia Plateau is Kenya's best-kept wildlife secret — a vast highland wilderness north of Mount Kenya where private conservancies and community ranches have created one of Africa's most successful models of wildlife conservation outside of national parks. Laikipia is home to the second-largest population of elephants in Kenya, the largest population of Grevy's zebra in the world, significant populations of both black and white rhino, African wild dog, and the rare and elusive Jackson's hartebeest.

What makes Laikipia extraordinary is the safari experience itself — because you're on private conservancy land rather than in a national park, activities that are impossible elsewhere in Kenya are the norm here. Night game drives, bush walks with armed rangers, horseback safaris, mountain biking through the wilderness, and camel trekking are all available. The conservancies' exclusive nature means you'll rarely encounter another vehicle — a far cry from the crowded roads of the Maasai Mara during peak season.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1
Nairobi → Laikipia — Arrival & Afternoon Game Drive

Fly from Nairobi Wilson Airport to your conservancy's private airstrip (approx. 45 minutes) or drive via Nyahururu (approx. 4 hours). Arrive at your exclusive conservancy lodge — small, intimate, and set in the heart of the wilderness. Afternoon game drive in a private 4WD — watch for elephant herds, Grevy's zebra, reticulated giraffe, and the conservancy's resident lion pride. Sundowner drinks in the bush. Dinner under the stars at the lodge's outdoor boma.

Day 2
Rhino Tracking & Bush Walk

Dawn game drive focused on rhino tracking — Laikipia is one of the best places in Kenya to see both black and white rhino, with dedicated ranger teams monitoring them daily. After breakfast, a guided bush walk with armed conservancy rangers — walk through the African wilderness at ground level, learning to track animals by footprints and signs, and experiencing the bush in a completely different way to vehicle game drives. Afternoon at leisure — optional camel trek or horseback ride (additional cost). Night game drive after dinner — spot nocturnal predators, African wild cat, and the elusive aardvark.

Day 3
Community Conservancy Visit & Wild Dog Tracking

Morning drive with your guide focused on finding Laikipia's African wild dog packs — one of the most endangered carnivores in Africa, with the Laikipia Plateau hosting one of Kenya's few remaining viable populations. Afternoon visit to a neighbouring community conservancy project — meet the Maasai and Samburu community members who are at the heart of Laikipia's conservation success, and learn how wildlife tourism directly benefits local families and schools. Evening campfire with your guide sharing stories of the bush.

Day 4
Final Morning Drive → Return to Nairobi

Final dawn game drive — one last chance for those wild dog and rhino sightings before departure. After breakfast, fly or drive back to Nairobi. Arrive Nairobi by early afternoon.
