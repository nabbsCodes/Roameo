2-Day Hell's Gate Cycling Safari

📅 Nov 21 – 22, 2026 ⏱ 2 Days / 1 Night 👥 Group Tour 🇰🇪 Kenya

Tour Overview

Hell's Gate National Park is unlike any other wildlife destination in Kenya — and possibly in all of Africa. It is one of the only national parks on the continent where visitors can cycle and walk freely among wildlife, without fences, without vehicles, and without a guide requirement. Named after a narrow break in the cliffs that was once an outlet for an ancient lake, Hell's Gate is a dramatic landscape of towering red-rock cliffs, deep volcanic gorges, geothermal steam vents, and open grassland where zebra, buffalo, giraffe, and warthog roam freely around cyclists and walkers.

The park famously inspired the landscape of Disney's The Lion King — and cycling through the dramatic gorge with giraffe watching you pass and steam rising from the geothermal vents is one of the most surreal and exhilarating experiences in Kenya. Hell's Gate is just 90 kilometres from Nairobi and sits next to Lake Naivasha, making it easy to combine both into a single weekend escape — which is exactly what this 2-day package does.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1
Nairobi → Hell's Gate — Cycling Safari & Gorge Walk

Depart Nairobi at 7:00am by road (approx. 90 minutes via the Mai Mahiu escarpment with spectacular Rift Valley views). Arrive at Hell's Gate National Park main gate. Collect your bicycles and helmets and begin the cycling safari — ride through the park's open grasslands past herds of zebra, giraffe, buffalo, hartebeest, and warthog. The 22km cycling circuit takes approximately 3–4 hours at a leisurely pace with stops for wildlife and photos. After lunch at the picnic site, descend on foot into the dramatic Hell's Gate Gorge — a narrow slot canyon carved by ancient rivers through layers of volcanic rock, with geothermal steam vents hissing from the cliff walls. A guided gorge walk with a park ranger takes approximately 2 hours. Transfer to your Lake Naivasha lodge for the evening. Sundowner drinks by the lake. Dinner at the lodge.

Day 2
Lake Naivasha Boat Ride & Crescent Island Walk → Nairobi

After breakfast, board a traditional wooden boat for a guided hippo-watching cruise on Lake Naivasha — the lake is home to a large hippo population and extraordinary birdlife including African fish eagle, pelican, and cormorant. Cross to Crescent Island for a guided walking safari among zebra, wildebeest, giraffe, and Thomson's gazelle — one of the only places in Kenya where you can walk freely alongside wildlife in the open. Return to the lodge for lunch before departing for Nairobi. Arrive Nairobi by mid-afternoon.
