6-Day Cape Town & Winelands Discovery

📅 Oct 3 – 8, 2026 ⏱ 6 Days / 5 Nights 👥 Group Tour 🇿🇦 South Africa

Tour Overview

Cape Town is consistently voted one of the world's most beautiful cities — and with Table Mountain rising dramatically behind a coastline where two oceans meet, it's easy to see why. This 6-day discovery takes you through the very best of the Cape: the iconic Table Mountain, the colourful Bo-Kaap neighbourhood, the historic V&A Waterfront, the famous Boulders Beach penguin colony, and the breathtaking Cape of Good Hope where the Atlantic and Indian Oceans converge.

Beyond the city, the Cape Winelands — Stellenbosch, Franschhoek, and Paarl — offer world-class wine tasting among vineyard estates and mountain scenery that rivals Tuscany. October is one of the best months to visit — spring in the Cape means wildflowers in bloom, clear skies, and perfect temperatures before the summer crowds arrive.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1
Nairobi → Cape Town — Arrival & V&A Waterfront

Fly from Nairobi to Cape Town International Airport (approx. 5 hours). Transfer to your hotel in the heart of Cape Town. Afternoon stroll along the V&A Waterfront — one of Africa's most vibrant harbour developments with restaurants, markets, and stunning views of Table Mountain. Welcome dinner at a waterfront seafood restaurant.

Day 2
Table Mountain & City Bowl Tour

Morning cable car ride to the top of Table Mountain (weather permitting) for panoramic views across the Cape Peninsula, the city, and both oceans. Afternoon guided tour of Cape Town's City Bowl — the colourful Bo-Kaap Malay Quarter, the Company's Garden, St. George's Cathedral, and the vibrant Long Street. Evening free time to explore Cape Town's renowned restaurant scene.

Day 3
Cape Peninsula — Boulders Beach & Cape of Good Hope

Full day Cape Peninsula scenic drive. Stop at Hout Bay harbour for a fresh fish lunch. Continue to Boulders Beach near Simon's Town to walk among the famous African penguin colony — one of only a few land-based penguin colonies in the world. Drive to the Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve and stand at Africa's south-western most point where the two oceans meet. Return to Cape Town via Chapman's Peak Drive — one of the world's most scenic coastal roads.

Day 4
Stellenbosch & Franschhoek Winelands

Drive into the Cape Winelands to the historic university town of Stellenbosch — South Africa's second oldest town and the heart of the Cape wine industry. Visit two award-winning wine estates for guided cellar tours and tastings. Lunch at a vineyard restaurant. Afternoon in Franschhoek — the Cape's culinary capital, founded by French Huguenot settlers, with charming village architecture and exceptional restaurants. Return to Cape Town in the evening.

Day 5
Robben Island & Kirstenbosch Gardens

Morning ferry to Robben Island — the island prison where Nelson Mandela was held for 18 of his 27 years in captivity. Guided tour of the prison led by a former political prisoner. Afternoon visit to Kirstenbosch National Botanical Garden on the eastern slopes of Table Mountain — one of the great botanic gardens of the world, at its most spectacular in spring. Farewell dinner at a restaurant on the slopes of Signal Hill.

Day 6
Leisure Morning → Return to Nairobi

Final morning at leisure — last views of Table Mountain, a coffee at a Cape Town café, or last-minute shopping at the Old Biscuit Mill market (Saturdays only). Transfer to Cape Town International Airport for return flight to Nairobi.
