10-Day Classic Europe Grand Tour

📅 Sep 1 – 10, 2026 ⏱ 10 Days / 9 Nights 👥 Group Tour 🌍 7 Countries

Tour Overview

The Classic Europe Grand Tour is Roameo's most ambitious and beloved international package — a sweeping 10-day journey through seven of Europe's most iconic countries, from the romance of Paris to the canals of Venice. This tour is designed for travellers who want to experience the very best of Europe in one seamless, expertly guided trip — without the stress of planning intercity trains, booking hotels across multiple countries, or navigating unfamiliar transport systems in different languages.

In ten days you will stand beneath the Eiffel Tower in Paris, taste Belgian chocolate in Brussels, cruise Amsterdam's famous canals, admire the Gothic grandeur of Cologne Cathedral, ride a cable car above the Swiss Alps in Lucerne, visit the tiny principality of Liechtenstein, explore the imperial palaces of Vienna, glide through Venice's Grand Canal by gondola, and end in the fashion capital of Milan. Seven countries, nine nights, and memories that will last a lifetime.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1
Nairobi → Paris, France — Arrival & Seine Cruise

Fly from Nairobi Jomo Kenyatta International Airport to Paris Charles de Gaulle (approx. 9–10 hours, direct or via one stop). Transfer to your central Paris hotel. Freshen up and head out for an evening Seine river cruise — gliding past Notre Dame Cathedral, the Louvre, and the Eiffel Tower as Paris lights up at dusk. Welcome dinner at a traditional Parisian brasserie.

Day 2
Paris, France — City Tour & Eiffel Tower

Full day exploring Paris. Morning guided tour of the Louvre Museum — home to the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and thousands of years of world art. Afternoon stroll along the Champs-Élysées from the Arc de Triomphe to the Place de la Concorde. Evening visit to the Eiffel Tower — ascend to the second floor for panoramic views over the City of Light. Dinner in the Montmartre district.

Day 3
Paris → Brussels, Belgium — Grand Place & Chocolate

Morning high-speed Thalys train from Paris Gare du Nord to Brussels (1 hour 20 minutes). Check in and head straight to the Grand Place — Brussels' magnificent central square, ringed by ornate Gothic guildhalls and rated one of the most beautiful squares in the world. Guided tour of the square and surrounding streets. Visit a traditional Belgian chocolate shop for a tasting session — Belgium produces over 220,000 tonnes of chocolate per year and its chocolatiers are considered the finest in the world. Evening at leisure in Brussels.

Day 4
Brussels → Amsterdam, Netherlands — Canals & Culture

Morning train to Amsterdam (approx. 2 hours). Afternoon guided canal cruise through Amsterdam's UNESCO-listed canal ring — 165 canals, 1,281 bridges, and some of the world's most beautiful 17th century merchant houses lining the waterways. Visit the Anne Frank House — the secret annex where Anne Frank and her family hid from the Nazis for two years during World War II. Evening free time in the Jordaan district — Amsterdam's most charming neighbourhood of independent boutiques, brown cafés, and candlelit restaurants.

Day 5
Amsterdam → Cologne, Germany — Cathedral & Rhine

Morning at leisure in Amsterdam — optional visit to the Van Gogh Museum (additional cost) before departure. Train to Cologne (approx. 2.5 hours). Visit the Cologne Cathedral — a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the finest examples of Gothic architecture in the world, taking over 600 years to complete. Evening riverside walk along the Rhine with views of the illuminated cathedral. Dinner at a traditional Cologne brewery restaurant — try the local Kölsch beer and Himmel un Ääd (heaven and earth) dish.

Day 6
Cologne → Lucerne, Switzerland — Alpine Cable Car

Train south through Germany into Switzerland, arriving in Lucerne (approx. 4 hours with scenic Rhine Valley views). Cross the famous Chapel Bridge — a covered wooden bridge dating from 1333 and one of Switzerland's most recognisable landmarks. Afternoon cable car ascent of Mount Pilatus (2,132m) for spectacular views across Lake Lucerne and the Swiss Alps. Dinner in Lucerne's beautifully preserved medieval old town.

Day 7
Lucerne → Vaduz, Liechtenstein → Innsbruck, Austria

Morning drive east through the Swiss Alps to Vaduz — the tiny capital of Liechtenstein, one of the world's smallest countries and one of only two doubly landlocked nations on earth. Photo stop at Vaduz Castle perched above the Rhine valley, and a chance to get your passport stamped at the Liechtenstein tourism office (a popular souvenir). Continue to Innsbruck, the Tyrolean capital of Austria — evening walk through the medieval old town past the famous Golden Roof.

Day 8
Vienna, Austria — Imperial Palaces & Coffee Houses

Train from Innsbruck to Vienna (approx. 4 hours). Vienna is one of Europe's great imperial capitals — afternoon guided tour of the magnificent Schönbrunn Palace, the summer residence of the Habsburg emperors with 1,441 rooms and a hilltop gloriette with panoramic city views. Visit St. Stephen's Cathedral in the heart of the old city. Experience a traditional Viennese coffee house — order a Melange (Viennese coffee) and a slice of Sachertorte. Evening classical music concert (optional, additional cost).

Day 9
Venice, Italy — Gondola & St Mark's Square

Train from Vienna to Venice (approx. 6 hours through the Dolomites). Arrive in one of the world's most unique and magical cities — Venice is built on 118 small islands connected by 400 bridges, with no cars or roads. Guided walking tour of St Mark's Square and the Doge's Palace. Gondola ride through the narrow side canals of Venice — passing under stone bridges, alongside crumbling Renaissance palaces, and through quiet neighbourhoods that feel unchanged for centuries. Farewell dinner at a canal-side Venetian restaurant.

Day 10
Milan, Italy — Cathedral & Departure

Train from Venice to Milan (approx. 2.5 hours). Morning visit to the magnificent Milan Cathedral (Duomo di Milano) — the world's largest Gothic cathedral, with 3,400 statues, 135 spires, and a rooftop terrace walkable above the city. Walk through the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II — one of the world's oldest and most beautiful shopping arcades. Transfer to Milan Malpensa Airport for return flight to Nairobi. Arrive Nairobi the following morning.
